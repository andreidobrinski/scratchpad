About Scratchpad

What is it?

It's a notes app that focuses on quickly jotting down a new idea. Check out the app here.

Why would I use it?

Scratchpad is made with the idea that it's better to note down an idea quickly and organize it later, rather than needing to organize first.

Picture a moment when you're talking with your friend in person and they recommend a TV show for you to watch. You may open up your notes app, find your “tv shows to watch” note, scroll to the bottom, add your show then save the note. Alternatively you can open Scratchpad, which always opens with a new note, record the show and save the note.

Scratchpad is built to save your ideas quickly, so that you can get back to what you were doing before.

How does it work?

Open the app and record ideas. No signup or login required. No internet required either since the app is built to for full offline support thanks to service workers.

I recommend using the app on your phone and adding the icon to your home screen on either your iOS or Android device.

What about my data, how is it stored and used?

Everything you record lives on your device, in your browser's local storage. No data is sent back to Scratchpad. In fact, Scratchpad is a client-only app and doesn't have its own servers.

This also means that clearing your browser's cache will remove all Scratchpad data.

See for yourself! The Scratchpad codebase is open source and can be found on GitHub.
